What is elder abuse and why should you care?

Elder abuse is a silent problem that robs seniors of their dignity, security, and—in some cases—costs them their lives. Elder abuse includes physical abuse, emotional abuse, sexual abuse, exploitation, neglect, and abandonment. It occurs when there is a reliance on the abuser with the expectation of trust, causing significant harm and stress to an older person. Perpetrators include children, other family members, and spouses—as well as staff at nursing homes, assisted living, and other facilities. Emerging evidence indicates that the prevalence of abuse of older people in both the community and in institutions have increased during the COVID-19 pandemic. A US study, for instance, suggests that rates in the community may have increased by as much as 84%
